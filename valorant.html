2. Valorant

❮ ❯

*Valorant* is a tactical first-person shooter developed by Riot Games, released in 2020. It features a 5v5 team-based format where players select from a diverse roster of agents, each with unique abilities that add strategic depth to the gameplay. The game’s primary mode, similar to *Counter-Strike*, involves one team attacking by planting a spike (bomb) while the other defends. Players must carefully manage their in-game economy to buy weapons and abilities, making every round a tactical challenge. The maps are designed with various lanes and verticality, encouraging coordinated team play and creative strategies. Riot Games frequently updates the game with new agents, maps, and balance changes to keep the experience fresh and competitive. Beyond its engaging gameplay, *Valorant* has established a strong presence in the esports scene. With international tournaments like *Valorant Champions* and the *VCT Masters*, the game has attracted professional teams and a massive audience. Its popularity is further boosted by the game's free-to-play model, making it accessible to a wide range of players. Riot Games also emphasizes community engagement, frequently collaborating with streamers and content creators. The game's detailed graphics, vibrant character designs, and immersive soundscapes contribute to its appeal. With continuous updates and a growing competitive scene, *Valorant* remains one of the most prominent titles in the FPS genre.
